    KubeCon NA 2019 CTF¶

    Introduction¶

    Welcome to the KubeCon NA 2019 Tutorial: Attacking and Defending Kubernetes Clusters: A Guided Tour Walkthrough Guide. Each participant will be given access to their own account with direct access to their own Kubernetes cluster environment. Follow along as we take on the role of two attacking personas looking to make some money and one defending persona working hard to keep the cluster safe and healthy.

    Getting Connected¶

    1. Get a "credential card" from one of the room assistants. Do not share these credentials with others unless you are planning on working together.

      A Note on Sharing Credentials

      For the best possible experience, every participant should interact with their own dedicated environment to avoid accidental contention when running commands that modify the environment. We encourage working together with your neighbor--each working in their own cluster.

    2. If you're not already in an Incognito/Private window, right-click this link and choose "Open Link in Icognito Window" (or similar option).

    3. Right-click the button below, choose "Open in New Tab", and sign in with your credentials.

    4. Accept the Terms:

    5. Accept the next set of Terms:

    6. Check "I Agree..." and then Start Cloud Shell:

    7. At the top of the Browser window, click Dismiss on the free GCP Trial banner if you see it.

    8. Once inside the Cloud Shell terminal, run: 

      ./setup.sh

      Use the Copy to Clipboard Feature

      Each terminal command block in this guide has a double-square icon on the far right side which automatically copies the content to your paste buffer to make things easier to follow along.

    9. Next, run: 

      kubectl get pods --all-namespaces

    10. If you see a pod listing, you are all set. Wait at this point until we work together as a group.
    11. If your neighbor needs assistance, please ask if they would like assistance and do so if they accept. If you are having even greater difficulty, please raise your hand for room assistant help.
